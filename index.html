Hello! My name is Sanjit Bhat and I'm currently a junior in Acton-Boxborough Regional High School. When I'm not in school, I usually spend most of my time doing research in the MIT PRIMES program.

My current research is in adversarial machine learning. I'm working with Dimitris Tsipras in the Madry Lab, creating experiments which take a principled approach toward deep learning. Our overall goals are to understand why and when neural networks work, in what situations they can be broken, and how to design more robust networks.

In the past, I worked with Albert Kwon and David Lu on a Computer Security project. Together, we studied anonymous networks such as Tor and created better attacks and defenses for Website Fingerprinting. My work was mainly on the attack, which used deep learning techniques to outperform all prior-art.
